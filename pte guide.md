## 1. Artículos — A / AN / THE

### Regla básica

|Artículo|Cuándo usar|Ejemplo|
|---|---|---|
|**a**|Antes de sonido consonántico|a university, a European country, a fact|
|**an**|Antes de sonido vocálico|an hour, an MBA, an universal fact ✗ → **a** universal fact ✓|
|**the**|Cosa específica ya mencionada o única|the government, the evidence above|
|∅ (sin artículo)|Plural genérico / abstracto|Degrees are important. Knowledge is power.|

### Errores frecuentes en PTE Writing

```
✗  it's not an universal fact
✓  it is not a universal fact

✗  the education is essential
✓  education is essential  (concepto abstracto general)

✗  a information
✓  the information / information  (incontable)

✗  I studied in university
✓  I studied at university / at a university
```

### Sustantivos incontables — NUNCA llevan "a/an"

`advice · information · knowledge · evidence · research · progress · work · traffic · luggage · equipment · furniture · news · money · time`

```
✗  a research / researches
✓  a piece of research / research (in general)

✗  an advice
✓  a piece of advice / some advice
```

## 3. Preposiciones — combinaciones frecuentes en el PTE

### Preposiciones de tiempo

```
in + año / mes / siglo       in 2024, in January, in the 21st century
on + día / fecha             on Monday, on 5 March
at + hora / festividad       at 9 am, at Christmas
for + duración               for three years, for a long time
since + punto de inicio      since 2010, since the introduction of...
during + período             during the pandemic, during childhood
```

### Preposiciones de lugar

```
in + ciudad / país / espacio cerrado     in Spain, in the room
at + punto / institución                  at university, at work, at the station
on + superficie / calle                   on the table, on Oxford Street
```

### Collocations con preposición fija

```
interested IN       dependent ON        responsible FOR
good AT             concerned ABOUT     result IN
lead TO             consist OF          refer TO
contribute TO       benefit FROM        differ FROM
```

### Error frecuente — "in his/her career" → género neutro

```
✗  if someone wants to succeed in his career
✓  if someone wants to succeed in their career
✓  if one wishes to succeed in one's career  (más formal)
```

## 4. Estructura del essay PTE — plantilla

El PTE espera una posición **clara y firme** desde el inicio. No es IELTS donde se puede discutir ambos lados sin concluir.

```
Párrafo 1 — Introducción + tesis (40–50 palabras)
  • Parafrasear el topic
  • Expresar tu posición claramente
  • [Opcional] indicar los dos puntos que desarrollarás

Párrafo 2 — Argumento principal (70–80 palabras)
  • Topic sentence (idea principal)
  • Elaboración
  • Ejemplo concreto
  • Consecuencia / por qué importa

Párrafo 3 — Argumento secundario o contraargumento (70–80 palabras)
  • Topic sentence
  • Elaboración
  • Ejemplo concreto
  • Refuerzo de tu posición

Párrafo 4 — Conclusión (30–40 palabras)
  • Reiterar la posición (NO copiar la intro)
  • Síntesis de los puntos
  • [Opcional] implicación o llamada a la reflexión
```

### Longitud recomendada

- Summarise Written Text: **1 sola oración**, 5–75 palabras
- Write Essay: **200–300 palabras** (mínimo 200 o penalización)

## 5. Introducción — frases de apertura

### Para "agree / disagree"

```
It is widely argued that [topic]. While there is some merit in this view,
I firmly believe that [tu posición], as [razón breve].

The notion that [topic] is a matter of considerable debate.
In my view, [tu posición] for the reasons outlined below.

Although [concesión menor], it is my contention that [posición principal].
```

### Para "discuss both views"

```
There are those who maintain that [vista A], while others contend that [vista B].
After careful consideration, I am inclined to support [tu preferencia].
```

### Para "advantages / disadvantages"

```
[Topic] presents both opportunities and challenges for [affected group].
On balance, the benefits of [X] outweigh the drawbacks, provided that [condición].
```

### Reported speech

```

```

## 11. Puntuación formal

### Coma

- Después de conector inicial: `However, ... / Therefore, ... / In addition, ...`
- En oraciones de relativo no restrictivas: `Education, which is a fundamental right, must be accessible.`
- Para separar ítems en una lista de 3+: `reading, writing, and listening`

### Punto y coma

- Unir dos oraciones relacionadas sin conjunción: `The policy was controversial; many stakeholders objected.`

### Dos puntos

- Antes de una lista o explicación: `There are three key factors: access, quality, and funding.`

### NO usar en PTE Writing formal

- Contracciones: `it's → it is / don't → do not / can't → cannot`
- Signos de exclamación: `!`
- Puntos suspensivos: `...`

## 12. Plantilla de essay completa — ejemplo

**Topic:** _Some people believe that practical experience is more valuable than academic qualifications. To what extent do you agree?_

---

**Introducción**

It is frequently argued that hands-on experience holds greater value than formal academic credentials. While I acknowledge that practical skills are indispensable in certain fields, I firmly believe that academic qualifications provide an essential theoretical foundation that practical experience alone cannot replicate.

**Párrafo 2 — Argumento principal (a favor de la posición)**

To begin with, academic qualifications equip individuals with the theoretical knowledge necessary to understand complex professional challenges. In the field of medicine, for instance, a practitioner must thoroughly comprehend anatomy, pharmacology, and diagnostic procedures before treating patients. Without this grounding, practical exposure could prove not only ineffective but potentially harmful. Furthermore, qualifications are widely recognised by employers and professional bodies, serving as an objective benchmark of competence.

**Párrafo 3 — Concesión + refuerzo**

Admittedly, there are disciplines in which applied skills carry considerable weight. Business management, for example, demands the ability to navigate real-world scenarios that academic curricula may not fully replicate. Nevertheless, even in such fields, formal education provides the analytical frameworks and critical thinking skills that enable professionals to make informed decisions. It is therefore evident that qualifications and experience complement, rather than replace, one another.

**Conclusión**

In conclusion, while practical experience undeniably enhances professional competence, academic qualifications remain a crucial prerequisite in most disciplines. A balanced approach that integrates both theoretical knowledge and applied skills is, in my view, the most effective pathway to professional excellence.

## 13. Describir una imagen  — ejemplo

The image is a [bar chart / line graph / pie chart] showing [topic].

The highest figure can be seen in [category/year] at [number/percentage], whereas the lowest figure is observed in [category/year] at [number/percentage].

Overall, the chart shows a steady increase / decrease / upward trend / downward trend.

Alternative conclusions:

- Overall, the graph indicates a positive trend.
    
- Overall, the chart shows significant fluctuations over time.
    
- Overall, smartphones and laptops account for the majority of the total.
    
- Overall, the data highlights a clear difference between the categories.
